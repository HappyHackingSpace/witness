## Name
Witness - Web Evidence Capture

## Short Description (132 chars max)
Capture, hash, and preserve web evidence with SHA-256 integrity chains and RFC 3161 trusted timestamps. For OSINT, legal, and research.

## Detailed Description

Witness captures everything you see online, preserves it with cryptographic integrity, and lets you prove it was real. Web pages disappear. Social media posts get deleted. Evidence vanishes. Witness makes sure you have proof.

FEATURES:

* Automatic Capture - Every page you visit while recording is captured automatically. MHTML preserves the full page (HTML, CSS, images, scripts). A viewport or full-page screenshot is taken alongside it. No manual "save" button. Just browse.

* Forensic Integrity - Every capture is hashed with SHA-256. Each hash links to the previous one, forming a tamper-evident chain. Break one link and the entire chain flags it. RFC 3161 trusted timestamps from FreeTSA.org and DigiCert provide third-party proof of when evidence was captured.

* Cases and Organization - Group captures into investigation cases. Switch between cases from the header. Tag captures, add notes, and filter by any combination. Search across all captured page content and metadata.

* Selectors and Smart Detection - Define keywords, usernames, emails, phone numbers, crypto addresses, or custom regex patterns. Every captured page is automatically scanned against all active selectors. Findings are aggregated across your entire evidence set.

* Annotations - Mark up screenshots without altering the original evidence. Highlight key areas, redact sensitive information, draw arrows, circle elements. Annotations are stored separately so the original screenshot remains forensically intact.

* Export - ZIP packages for file-based workflows. WARC (ISO 28500) for archives and legal proceedings. HTML reports with screenshots, hashes, timestamps, and investigator notes.

WHO IS THIS FOR:

* OSINT investigators documenting online investigations with an auditable trail
* Journalists capturing sources that can disappear at any moment
* Legal professionals presenting web-based evidence in court
* Cybersecurity analysts researching threats, phishing campaigns, and infrastructure
* Human rights researchers documenting abuses that governments try to erase
* Anyone who has ever thought "I should have saved that page"

PRIVACY:

All data stays in your browser. No external servers. No accounts. No tracking. The only outbound requests are RFC 3161 timestamp queries to public TSA servers, and those contain only a SHA-256 hash, not your data. Fully open source.
